Module 1

A short reading display of a poem by Tenchi Tennō, from the series One Hundred Poems Explained by the Nurse (Hyakunin isshu uba ga etoki) 百人一首　うはかゑとき　天智天皇.

DESCRIPTION
Project README and code available on  GitHub
  • Aki no ta no Kario no io no Toma wo arami Waga koromode wa Tsuyu ni nure-tsutsu Coarse the rush-mat roof Sheltering the harvest-hut Of the autumn rice-field And my sleeves are growing wet With the moisture dripping through This ESP32 display spells out a haiku (俳句)--a type of Japanese poem that follows an unrhymed three-line, seventeen-syllable format. It was written sometime in the Edo period (1615–1868) in Japan. The display of the lines within the haiku follows an unusual rhythm to complement the nature of the unrhymed format of haikus. Additionally, the font of each line, as well as the placement in some instances don’t match the previous thereby creating a unique legibility to the poem.

  • The classroom setting of our showcase made me choose a haiku since it’s short and each line can stand on its own for any viewer looking at it for a short period of time. The vision was to create a flashing, short, and pleasant visual reading experience that matches the format of a haiku on paper.

    For future references, I would make sure that the orientation of the display follows the orientation of the actual showcase so that the viewer may read the text easier. I had some technical difficulties with the size and color of the font, and in the future I would take into account the setting of the showcase a lot more (such as the lighting, the distance between the ESP32 and the viewer, etc) so ensure that the display is legible. To this note, I would add that changes in font would also require changes in space lining between each poem line. Should the font get bigger and the space lining not change, the poem lines will stack on top of each other and make it difficult to read the previous line.
